          <h2 style="font-family:'Georgia',serif;color:var(--ink)">About the Sensor</h2>

          <p style="color:var(--muted)">The Smart Sub-Q Dot is a tiny, rice-grain-sized implant designed to measure 2–3 high-value physiological signals continuously. It can be configured for cardiopulmonary, metabolic, or ortho/post-op use and transmits securely to a patient phone and clinician dashboard.</p>


          <h3 style="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:6px;color:#112">Why this approach?</h3>

          <ul style="color:var(--muted);margin-left:18px">

            <li><strong>Low patient burden:</strong> no charging, no daily interaction.</li>

            <li><strong>Clinical value:</strong> measures signals non-capturable by wearables (local tissue oxygenation, intracardiac trends, local biomarkers).</li>

            <li><strong>Workflow friendly:</strong> integrates into clinician dashboards and EHR via secure APIs.</li>

          </ul>


          <h3 style="margin-top:12px;color:#112">Typical configurations</h3>

          <div style="display:grid;gap:10px;margin-top:8px">

            <div class="card" style="padding:12px">

              <strong>Cardiopulmonary</strong>

              <div style="color:var(--muted);font-size:14px">StO₂ (local tissue oxygenation), local temperature, HR/HRV trends — early detection of HF decompensation and respiratory infections.</div>

            </div>

            <div class="card" style="padding:12px">

              <strong>Metabolic</strong>

              <div style="color:var(--muted);font-size:14px">Continuous glucose ± lactate and pH — early metabolic instability and sepsis risk signalling.</div>

            </div>

            <div class="card" style="padding:12px">

              <strong>Ortho / Post-Op</strong>

              <div style="color:var(--muted);font-size:14px">Local temperature, pH, mechanical strain — early prosthetic infection and healing assessment.</div>

            </div>

          </div>
